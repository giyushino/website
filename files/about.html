ABOUT ME

Allan Zhang

Math of Comp

I'm Allan, a frosh studying applied mathematics, but I am looking to tack on another major. I'm currently pretty interested in machine learning, specifically the fields of computer vision and LMMs. These days, I've been looking a lot into the optimization of model inference and mechanistic interpretability. However, I know myself well enough to know that my interests can change at the drop of a hat. While I'm not exactly sure what I'll be doing in the future, I am pretty keen on going to graduate school and conducting research related to machine learning.

Currently, I'm working as a research assistant at BigML @ UCLA, where I'm currently helping a graduate student study where and why multimodal models fail at spatial reasoning tasks. It has been an extremely fulfilling experience and I have learned a lot about academia and working in a team. When I'm not working on research or school work, I enjoy reading, writing, and coding. Since I only started learning to code a few months ago, I've been trying to code (in Neovim obviously) as much as possible. While I have mostly been working with Python and machine learning, I hope to expand my skillset to C++ (maybe gamedev?).

My current project involves testing different methods of finetuning an LLM to become an optimal math agent. I have been experimenting and studying how datasets, model architecture, compute power, prompting, low rank adaption, and other factors can affect the model's performance. I've also been spending a lot of time working with small CNNs and experimenting with different methods of training them. I have some really promising results that have come from "pseudo-evolution", and I will be doing a technical write up some time in the future
